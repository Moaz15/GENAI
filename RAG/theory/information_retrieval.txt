Classical IR systems (TF‑IDF, BM25) rely on counting words (“terms”) in documents. Before you can count reliably, you must define:

What is a “term”?
A term is the normalized token you index, typically produced by a pipeline:

Normalization: lowercasing, Unicode normalization (optional), removing punctuation.
Tokenization: splitting text into tokens (often words).
(Optional) Stopword removal: remove very common words like the, is, of to reduce noise/size.
(Optional) Stemming/Lemmatization: map variants to a common form (e.g., connect, connected, connecting → connect).

Your choices here affect:

vocabulary size
matching behavior (recall/precision)
scoring quality

What statistics do TF‑IDF/BM25 need?

Even before building an inverted index, you can compute key document-level statistics:

Document length (dl): number of tokens in a document after processing.
Collection size (N): number of documents.
Average document length (avgdl):
[ avgdl = \frac{1}{N}\sum_{d} dl(d) ] BM25 uses dl and avgdl to normalize term frequency by document length._
Also useful now (and required later):

Term Frequency (tf) within a document: count of a term in that doc (used by TF‑IDF and BM25).
Document Frequency (df): number of documents containing the term (needed for IDF later).
You can compute df in milestone 1, but it’s also naturally computed when building the inverted index in milestone 2.

what tokenization is in IR
In classical IR, everything starts with turning raw text into a sequence of terms that can be counted and matched.

A tokenizer/analyzer defines:

What counts as a term (word boundaries, punctuation, numbers, hyphens)
Normalization (lowercasing, handling accents, etc.)
(Optional) Stopword removal (drop extremely common words)
(Optional) Stemming/lemmatization (reduce word variants)
These choices directly affect:

Recall (can we match relevant docs?)
Example: “MRI” vs “mri” → lowercasing helps.
Precision (do we match too many irrelevant docs?)
Removing stopwords can reduce noise.
Index size (vocabulary + postings size)
Normalization and stopwords reduce size.
Scoring behavior for TF‑IDF/BM25
Tokenization affects tf/df counts, therefore ranking.
